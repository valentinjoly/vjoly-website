Wild potatoes (Solanum sect. Petota) form a large taxon gathering 196 species distributed in Latin America, from New Mexico to Patagonia {% cite BravoAlmonacid2012 %}. My PhD project aims at understanding their reproductive isolation, i.e. the mechanisms by which they avoid interspecific hybridization {% cite Ashkenazi2001 %}.

I am especially interested in Solanum chacoense, a wild potato relative of agronomical importance, and three of its closest relatives in the phylogeny: S. gandarillasii, S. tarijense, and S. microdontum {% cite Chen2014 Qin2005 %}.
