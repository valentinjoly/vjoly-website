Reproductive isolation

Wild potatoes (Solanum sect. Petota) form a large taxon gathering 196 species distributed in Latin America, from New Mexico to Argentina. My PhD project aims at understanding their reproductive isolation, i.e. the mechanisms by which they avoid interspecific hybridization.

In order to narrow down on a more reasonable number of species, we chose one model species —Solanum chacoense, a wild potato relative of agronomical importance— and three of its closest relatives in the phylogeny: S. gandarillasii, S. tarijense, and S. microdontum.
